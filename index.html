This is a time table for Alan Turing

  • His Early Life
    • 1912 - Turing was born in London, England.
    • 1926 - Enrolled into Sherborn Middle School and showed great talents in science and maths.
    • 1927 - He wrote a book to help his mother to understand Albert Eistein's theory of relativity. He has already showed that he had a mind of great capacity.
  • His Life in Science
    • 1931 - He was admiited to Cambridge and earned a scholarship for excellent maths skills.
    • 1936 - He became one of the most reknowened graduates in King's College.
    • 1937 - He published a paper about the World-famous "Turing Machine". He also published some other to make the theory of computer more reliable.
    • 1939 - He got his Ph.D in Princeton. What he found there also had a profound influence on the modern computing theory. This year, he also helped decode the Nazi's coding system.
  • After WW2
    • 1945 - He received his hornor for great contribution during the world war.
    • 1948 - He worked in Manchester as a mathmetician and a computer scientist.
    • 1950 - He published something related to AI, and he also invented his famous "Turing Test" for AI.
    • 1952 - He got terribly persecuted for being homosexual.
    • 1954 - Found dead in his house. Later investigation suggested that it was suicide. He left the famous half-biten apple.
